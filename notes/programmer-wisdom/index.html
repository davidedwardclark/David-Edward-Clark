Programmer Wisdom

A series of insights obtained from watching programmers at work.

Ryan Singer

  • Solve the problem on paper first.
  • Solve the most difficult screen first.

Neven Mrgan

  • Before you show people a prototype make sure it looks good. The details of the design must be fleshed out.
  • Start with the screen people spend 90% of their time on. Once that is established the rest of the app will come easy.

Eric Lindvall

  • Be slow and methodical.

Zed Shaw

  • Take frequent breaks for mind & body.
  • Its not about the code – its your understanding of the problem that matters.
  • No one creates perfect code off the bat. You start by getting something working and then you improve upon it.

Tim Caswell

  • When spiraling out of control collect your thoughts and think through the problem before continuing.
