Pragmatic Thinking & Learning

A series of quotes and paraphrases from Pragmatic Thinking & Learning by Andy Hunt.

  • Only remember things that matter. Keep the rest stored outside of your brain.
  • Make time to allow for your thoughts to marinate.
  • Shut your eyes and focus on breathing for ten minutes, three times a day.
  • Learn to pay attention.
  • The most important thing we need to learn is to manage focus.
  • Imagine yourself writing, designing and coding successfully: groove your mind for success.
  • Imagine your success.
  • Give yourself permission to fail: it’s the path to success.
  • Explore, invent and apply in your environment – safely.
  • Allow for a ton of iteration and failure.
  • Always consider context. Everything is connected.
  • Keep practicing in order to remain expert.
  • Cultivate intuition.
  • Harness your experience.
  • Realize the importance of context and of observing situational patterns.
  • Learn by synthesis as well as analysis.
  • Attractive things are easier to use.
  • Rewire your brain with belief and constant practice.
  • Add sensory experience to engage more of your brain.
  • Lead with your right brain, follow with your left.
  • Write freely, revise sober.
  • Create a shitty first draft.
  • Perfectionism is the voice of the oppressor.
  • Extend ideas past there breaking points.
  • Step away from the keyboard to solve hard problems.
