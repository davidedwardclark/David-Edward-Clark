Tools for Design & Coding

Two years ago I published a short piece on the tools I use to design and develop. A few things have changed since then. I replaced my late 2010 13" Macbook Air with a late 2013 15" Macbook Pro Retina. I did this mainly because I value the retina display more than I do portability. Another large change was the move away from Linode for hosting to a combination of Amazon S3 & Digital Ocean. There are two kinds of sites I need hosted: a) flat html sites like the one you are on and b) node backed dynamic sites. S3 is great for hosting flat sites & Digital Ocean is a virtual private server (VPS) which I use to host dynamic sites requiring a backend. Digital Ocean has the best VPS inteface I've ever used. Here is my current setup:

15" MacBook Pro Retina

  • OSX Terminal for command line UNIX.
  • Google Chrome & DevTools for browsing and developing.
  • iA Writer for writing.
  • Adobe Photoshop CC for design.
  • Sublime Text 3 for writing code.
  • Panic Transmit for sFTP'ing & S3 access.
  • iTunes for media management.
  • Parallels & Windows 7 for browser testing & playing games.
  • SuperDuper! for hard drive backups.

iPad Air

  • Comic Zeal for reading comics.
  • iBooks for reading books.
  • Paper by FiftyThree for drawing.
  • iA Writer for writing.
  • Instapaper for reading saved articles.
  • Google Drive for document management.
  • Web version of Gmail.

iPhone 5s

  • Web versions of Gmail & Tasks.
  • Tweetbot for Twitter.
  • Reeder for RSS.
  • Instapaper for reading saved articles.

Paper & Pens
