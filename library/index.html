Books

  • Universal Principles of Design

    An overview of design principles each with their own description and visualisation. Cites the definitive work on each subject.

  • Visual Thinking for Design

    A guide to the science of human visual perception. How the brain processes visual info. Useful in backing up design decisions.

  • Geometry of Design

    Transparent overlays show the geometric shapes behind objects. Learn how to derive the golden section using basic geometry.

  • Logo Design Love

    Takes you through a step by step process in logo creation. Author offers up sage advice on how to work with clients.

  • The Elements of Typographic Style

    Bringhurst’s masterpiece. Worthy of repeat readings and careful study this is the definitive work on the subject of typography.

  • The Complete Manual of Typography

    A good companion to Bringhurst. Worth it for the section on justification and alignment.

  • Grid Systems in Graphic Design

    MÃ¼ller-Brockmann’s definitive work on grid systems. A complete system for laying out type on a grid which become known as the Swiss style.

  • Making and Breaking the Grid

    A thorough review of the many ways a grid can be created and broken. More accessible than Brockmann – a good starter on the subject.

  • Understanding Comics

    Explores the mechanics of sequential art. This is for anyone who tells stories visually. McCloud truly is the Artistotle of comics.

  • The Art of Game Design

    Author employs different lenses as he constructs his philosophy of game design. A range of fields shed light on the subject.

  • The Elements of Color

    In depth explanation of colours and their effects on eachother. Considered a definitive work on the subject. Worth careful study.

  • Pantone Guide to Communicating with Color

    Hundreds of colour combinations. Great place to go if your having trouble choosing the right colour.

  • The Mythical Man Month

    Timeless principles and deep insights into managing complex software development projects. Excellent analogies throughout.

  • The Pragmatic Programmer

    Not specific to any language the authors condense years of experience and use pseudo code to teach best practices.

  • Pragmatic Thinking & Learning

    “Learn by synthesis as well as analysis.” This book is packed with techniques for harnessing your brains power.

  • The Passionate Programmer

    Stuffed full of great advice on how to thrive as a programmer. Like the others it isn’t programming language specific but advice driven.

Periodicals

  • CODEX

    There is nothing greater than sitting down with a copy of CODEX and a warm cup of coffee. It makes my heart sing. Print is alive & well.

  • 8 Faces

    Interviews with people who design & use type. Elliot has put his heart and soul into this publication and I look forward to each issue.

Documentaries

  • Helvetica

    The first in Hustwitt’s design trilogy this film explores typography. Contains excellent interviews with H&FJ and Erik Spiekermann.

  • Objectified

    The second in Hustwitt’s trilogy looks at industrial design: clocks, CD players & computers. Great interview with Johnny Ive.

  • Urbanized

    The final in the trilogy. Here he focuses on our built environment and how changes to the urban landscape effect us.

  • The Visual Language of Herbert Matter

    A showcase of Herbert Matter’s work which was a combination of design, photography and film.

  • BBS the Documentary

    The story of the life and death of bulletin board systems (BBS) – a popular communication technology that predated the internet.

  • Art & Copy

    Fascinating look into the world of advertising agencies. Looks at the people who disrupted the industry back in the 1960’s.

  • Get Lamp

    A documentary about the glory days of the text adventure game. Shines light on a distant time that only few got to experience.
