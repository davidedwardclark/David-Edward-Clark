Books

  • The Elements of Typographic Style

    Worthy of repeat readings and careful study this is the definitive work on the subject of typography.

  • Understanding Comics

    Food for the visual story teller. Scott McCloud's classic is an exploration of the mechanics of sequential art.

  • The Art of Game Design

    Lenses are used to build a philosophy of game design. The game maker as well as the film maker and story teller can benefit from this.

  • Visual Thinking for Design

    The science of how our brain processes visual information. Useful for backing up design decisions.

  • The Elements of Color

    An exploration of how colours work, their interaction with each other and how we perceive the result.

  • Universal Principles of Design

    Design principles described and visualised along with a citation of the definitive work on the subject.

  • Making and Breaking the Grid

    A thorough introduction to grids in visual design. Covers the many ways they can be created and broken.

  • Detail in Typography

    A fascinating look at the atomic elements that make up a block of text including letters, words, lines and the spacing in between.

  • Logo Design Love

    Worth it for the demonstration of process in logo creation. Offers excellent advice on working with clients as well.

  • Geometry of Design

    Transparent overlays show the geometric shapes behind objects. Learn how to derive the golden section using basic geometry.

  • The Mythical Man Month

    Timeless principles on the management of complex software development projects. Excellent analogies throughout.

  • The Pragmatic Programmer

    Years of author experience condensed into a few best practices. The best book in its category. Not specific to any programming language.

  • Pragmatic Thinking & Learning

    “Learn by synthesis as well as analysis.” Packed with techniques for harnessing your brain’s power.

  • The Passionate Programmer

    Full of advice on how to thrive as a programmer. Not specific to any programming language.

Periodicals

  • CODEX

    The journal of typography. For “everyone who has any interest in fonts, graphic design, lettering, and typography.”

  • 8 Faces

    Interviews with people who design & use type. Elliot has put his heart and soul into this publication and I look forward to each issue.

Documentaries

  • Helvetica

    The first in Hustwitt’s trilogy this entry explores typography. Excellent interviews with H&FJ and Erik Spiekermann.

  • Objectified

    Second in the trilogy this entry focuses on industrial design: clocks, CD players & computers. Great interview with Johnny Ive.

  • Urbanized

    The final in the trilogy. Here he focuses on our built environment and how changes to the urban landscape affect us.

  • The Visual Language of Herbert Matter

    A showcase of Herbert Matter’s work which was a combination of design, photography and film.
